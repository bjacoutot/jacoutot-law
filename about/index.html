Counsel at the Intersection of Law & the Digital Age

Bryan F. Jacoutot is a litigation attorney at Clark Hill PLC in Atlanta, Georgia, where he focuses on election law, cryptocurrency and Bitcoin law, and general civil litigation. He brings to each matter a rare combination of deep legal knowledge and firsthand technical understanding of the subjects at issue.

In election law, Bryan has represented candidates, officeholders, political parties, and government bodies in disputes spanning redistricting, campaign finance, ballot access, and constitutional rights. He litigates in both state and federal courts and has briefed appellate matters before the Eleventh Circuit.

In the cryptocurrency space, Bryan is among a small number of attorneys who understands Bitcoin at the protocol level. He has studied Bitcoin since its early years, written for Bitcoin Magazine, and been cited in legal circles for his analysis of the Howey Test as applied to digital assets. His cryptocurrency practice spans regulatory compliance, corporate formation, platform counseling, and litigation.

Beyond his specialties, Bryan maintains an active general civil litigation practice, handling commercial disputes, constitutional claims, and appellate matters in courts throughout Georgia and the Southeast.

He holds a B.A. in History from Auburn University (2011) and a J.D. from Georgia State University College of Law (2014), and is admitted in Georgia and before the United States District Courts for the Northern, Middle, and Southern Districts of Georgia, as well as the Eleventh Circuit Court of Appeals.
